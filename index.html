StreamHopper

Streaming service recommender app

According to a 2019 Deloitte survey, 47% of consumers felt overwhelmed with the number of streaming services available to them. My team built a ML recommender to address this problem. Users fill out a 3-minute survey, allowing us to cluster them with similar users to predict the content they'd most enjoy. We then recommend the streaming service with the most preferred content for that user. Check it out here.


Detecting Fraudulent Job Postings

Classification project submitted to DS4A Women's Summit 2020

As part of our submission to the DS4A Women's Summit 2020, my team modeled highly unbalanced fraudulant job postings data. Using LDA topic modeling on job titles, we detected topic clusters most associated with fraudulent postings. In these clusters, keywords like “entry”, “data”, and “home” are most likely to be mentioned together.


Does Ridesharing Increase Road Fatalities?

Data visualization using Python and QGIS

Our visualizations depict a general trend of increased accident fatalities after ridesharing was introduced in SF, SJ and LA. Fatalities tend to correlate with ridesharing peak hours and happen in the areas with the most pick-up/ drop-off activity. Using NLP, we also detected a sharp increase in phrases like "Celllular Phone" and "Using Device" in the accident documentation.


A Historical Look at CO2 Emissions and Temperature Anomaly

Climate data visualizer app using R Shiny

The purpose of this interactive climate data application is to overlay historical data on CO2 emissions and temperature anomaly in a cohesive and interesting way. The app contains a regression plot, a spiralized graph and a world map. The displays were designed to highlight the exploding upward trend in both metrics, while inviting the user to explore different parameters.
